Open Space Accessibility in Dhaka City

Objective of this Study

The benchmark for accessibility is an important aspect for any city. Arguably more so for a developing city like Dhaka. It is difficult to allocate open space facilities and plan out future extensions without any prior knowledge on accessibility. This research was done to assess the current accessibility to public open spaces. The interactive choropleth map below shows the accessibility of different parts of Dhaka City. A higher score depicts better accessibility.

Measuring Process

Road network data similar to OpenStreetMaps was used to generate a network dataset, which was then used to evaluate the accessibility. The Travel Distance from each residential block to public open space was measured via this network dataset. An Accessibility score was then assigned to the census tracts depending on how well the Travel Distance was on average.
A decay equation was used for the scoring process. This equation made sure the score was between 1 to 10 points, ensuring fair comparison between census tracts.

Accessibility Index, Ai = 10 eβd[i,j]

i) Where, Ai is the Accessibility score for a building block i;
ii) D[i,j] is the Travel Distance between open space j which is closest to the building block i;
iii) β = 0.00085 for walking distances calculated in meters. It is the exponent that controls the rate of the decay.

Note that none of the census tracts scored over 8, which is why we don’t see any 9’s or 10’s in the interactive map.


Usage of Network-based Distance over Euclidean Distance

A network-based approach mimics the actual pedestrian movement and follows the street network to measure the distance. Euclidean Distance, otherwise known as the crow-fly distance, is measured by a straight-line. Although it is simpler, the results are less precise.
